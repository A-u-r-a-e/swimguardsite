Our Story

SwimGuard was founded by two high school students at Shanghai American School with a simple but powerful belief: every pool should have access to life-saving technology, regardless of budget.

We've seen the unspoken dangers of indoors pools ourselves, with in-pool drowning causing 300,000 deaths annually and 30 people every hour worldwide. We saw Shanghai's 1,094 licensed pools serving 7 million summer visitors, yet struggling with impractical detection systems so expensive that most facilities wouldn't bother to even consider.

Combining our expertise in artificial intelligence, computer vision, and business strategy, we set out to create a drowning detection solution that breaks down the barriers of cost and complexity. SwimGuardian is the result of countless hours of research, development, and a deep commitment to making pool safety accessible to everyone. We achieved 91% detection accuracy with 100% true positive rate while keeping internal hardware expenses to just RMB 230 per unit.

The Team

Ben Qiu

Technical Lead & Co-Founder

Ben is the technical lead and chief engineer of SwimGuard. With extensive programming experience, he leads both the computer science club and the programming division of the robotics team at Shanghai American School.

His background in AI research includes work with transformers in arithmetic and training algorithms. Ben developed SwimGuardian's innovative three-stage architecture using YOLOv8, YoloPose, and LSTM neural networks, along with proprietary coordinate repositioning and distance normalization functions that enable accurate detection in crowded pool environments.

Kaylee Huang

Marketing & Business Strategy Lead, Co-Founder

Kaylee is the chief strategy officer, leading SwimGuard's business development and market strategy. She brings expertise in business model evaluation and strategic decision-making to ensure our technology reaches the facilities that need it most.

Her experience includes completing evaluations and analyses of business models in different research projects, including work with Grameen China, and interning at a startup where she gained insight into how strategic decisions address market needs. Kaylee developed SwimGuard's tiered pricing model designed to make drowning detection accessible to budget-conscious public pools across Shanghai and beyond.
